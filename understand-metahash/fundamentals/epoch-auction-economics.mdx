Every **360 blocks** (around 72 minutes), the system synchronizes:
miners submit α, validators run auctions, treasuries execute buybacks, and rewards flow back into the ecosystem.
This repeating process keeps the network in balance — predictable, transparent, and fair.

## 1. The Purpose of the Alpha Exchange

Each subnet in Bittensor produces **α** — its unique representation of value or output.
However, α by itself isn't liquid. It must be exchanged for tokens that can circulate (META or TAO).
The **Deterministic Alpha Exchange** provides the infrastructure for that conversion.

The exchange is not a market in the traditional sense.
There are no random trades, no bids racing against each other, and no price volatility.
Instead, MetaHash runs a **scheduled clearing process** every 360 blocks, where:

- Miners sell α in predictable auctions.
- Validators verify and commit bids to the chain.
- Treasuries redistribute proceeds automatically.

Each MV operates independently but follows the same 360-block schedule.

## 3. Deterministic Scoring and Fair Ranking

Each validator uses the same transparent formula to score bids:

```
Bid Score = α_amount × (1 - discount)
```

This means miners offering larger α or smaller discounts rank higher.
Bids are filled in order of score until a treasury's budget runs out.
Once rankings are finalized, they cannot change — guaranteeing fairness across all validators.

### Why This Matters
- No miner can jump ahead or bribe for position.
- No validator can modify a bid after submission.
- Everyone knows how the system behaves before it runs.

## 5. Treasury and Reward Flow

When an epoch ends, the liquidity collected by each treasury doesn't just stay there.
It is **redistributed automatically** through buybacks and airdrops.

### Treasury Actions
1. **Buybacks (75%)** —
   Used to stabilize liquidity and support META and subnet markets.
   Buybacks help balance prices and maintain network depth.
2. **Airdrops (25%)** —
   Sent directly to miners, α sellers, and participant wallets as yield.
   These encourage ongoing participation in the ecosystem.

### Validator Rewards
Validators also receive compensation for:
- Running and verifying auctions correctly.
- Maintaining on-chain and IPFS state.
- Keeping the exchange synchronized and deterministic.

## 6. The Liquidity Loop

Every epoch, MetaHash completes the same predictable cycle:

```
Miners sell α → Validators buy & verify → Treasuries redistribute → Miners receive yield → repeat
```

This continuous loop creates a **self-sustaining liquidity engine** where:
- α holders get instant exits.
- Validators earn from clearing trades.
- Treasuries support network growth.
- The cycle repeats every 360 blocks, forever.
