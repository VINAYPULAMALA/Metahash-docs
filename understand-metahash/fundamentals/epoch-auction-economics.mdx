Every **360 blocks** (around 72 minutes), the network enters a new exchange cycle.
Miners submit α, validators buy it, and treasuries redistribute value through buybacks and airdrops.
This predictable rhythm keeps liquidity flowing and the network in balance — block after block.

---

## 1. Why the Alpha Exchange Exists

Each subnet in Bittensor produces α — its digital output of work and intelligence.
But α by itself isn't liquid. It needs a mechanism to become spendable or stakable capital.

MetaHash provides that mechanism.
It transforms subnet production into network liquidity through:

- **Scheduled auctions** where miners sell α fairly and predictably.
- **Independent treasuries** managed by validators using their own budgets.
- **Cross-network flow** so liquidity can move freely between subnets.

## 3. Fair Scoring and Rule-Based Auctions

Every bid follows the same simple rule:
**the higher the α amount and the lower the discount, the stronger the bid.**

This scoring model ensures that:
- Bids are ranked transparently and can't be altered mid-auction.
- All validators use identical logic for fairness.
- Every participant knows how outcomes are decided before bidding.

## 5. Treasury and Reward Flow

When auctions close, treasuries redistribute proceeds back into the network automatically.
This process keeps liquidity circulating through every subnet.

### Treasury Actions
1. **Buybacks (75%)** — Support META and subnet liquidity, strengthening validator markets.
2. **Airdrops (25%)** — Reward miners, α sellers, and network participants directly.
3. **Reporting** — Record buybacks and airdrops on-chain and refresh budgets for the next epoch.

### Validator Incentives
Validators earn rewards for:
- Running auctions consistently and on time.
- Verifying bids fairly.
- Maintaining IPFS and on-chain records for transparency.

## 6. Network Interoperability: Connecting Subnets Through Liquidity

MetaHash doesn't just manage liquidity for SN73 — it links all of Bittensor's subnets together.
It acts as a **router** that allows α and liquidity to flow between subnet economies.

### Inbound Flow
- Subnets produce α as part of their local reward cycles.
- Miners can bring that α into MetaHash's auctions to convert it into META or TAO.
- Validators verify α's origin and quality before it's accepted.

### Outbound Flow
- After auctions, buyback liquidity and airdrops flow back into subnet treasuries.
- Subnets use this liquidity for staking, validator incentives, or growth programs.
- This keeps subnet economies funded and aligned with the wider Bittensor rhythm.

## 7. The MetaHash Liquidity Loop

The MetaHash liquidity cycle runs continuously, repeating every 360 blocks:

```
Miners sell α → Validators buy & verify → Treasuries redistribute → Subnets receive liquidity → repeat
```

This continuous loop creates a **self-sustaining liquidity engine** where:
- α holders get instant exits.
- Validators earn from clearing trades.
- Treasuries support network growth.
- The cycle repeats every 360 blocks, forever.
